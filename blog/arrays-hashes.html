Arrays & Hashes

The Difference between Arrays and Hashes

September 13, 2015

Arrays and hashes are both collections of data. They are important in storing lists of information, lookup data, or reference data. They are stored with an implicit order; however, they differ in the way that they are stored and accessed.

Arrays

An array acts as a list of data (e.g. strings, integers, floats, other arrays, etc.) Arrays are ordered in a way that each item is tied to an index number. This index number starts from the first item in the array 0..1,2,3.. to ..-4,-3,-2..-1 (last item).

Hashes

Hashes are collections of pairs of information, and each pair is made up of a key and value. These key-value pairs are stored with an implicit order, but are primarily accessed through their keys. Hashes effectively combine information that should be linked, and accessing information from a key makes for easier and faster reference.

Examples

There are different use cases for arrays and hashes. Arrays are versatile in that they can store any list of objects. However, in the case that you may need lookup values, a hash is the way to go.
